Trigonometry - Notes

Synopsis

Trigonometry is the study of triangles and the relationships between the lengths of their sides and the angles between those sides. Trigonometry is used to solve problems that involve angles and distances in two dimensions.

Important Points

  • The six trigonometric functions are sine (sin), cosine (cos), tangent (tan), cotangent (cot), secant (sec), and cosecant (csc).
  • The trigonometric ratios of an acute angle in a right triangle are defined by the length of the sides of the triangle.
  • The sine of an angle is equal to the opposite side divided by the hypotenuse.
  • The cosine of an angle is equal to the adjacent side divided by the hypotenuse.
  • The tangent of an angle is equal to the opposite side divided by the adjacent side.
  • The cotangent of an angle is equal to the adjacent side divided by the opposite side.
  • The secant of an angle is equal to the hypotenuse divided by the adjacent side.
  • The cosecant of an angle is equal to the hypotenuse divided by the opposite side.

Definitions

  • Trigonometry: The study of triangles and the relationships between the lengths of their sides and the angles between those sides.
  • Trigonometric Functions: The six functions used to describe the relationships between the sides and angles of a triangle.
  • Sine: The ratio of the length of the opposite side to the length of the hypotenuse.
  • Cosine: The ratio of the length of the adjacent side to the length of the hypotenuse.
  • Tangent: The ratio of the length of the opposite side to the length of the adjacent side.
  • Cotangent: The ratio of the length of the adjacent side to the length of the opposite side.
  • Secant: The ratio of the length of the hypotenuse to the length of the adjacent side.
  • Cosecant: The ratio of the length of the hypotenuse to the length of the opposite side.

Formulas

Sine: \(sin\theta = \frac{opposite}{hypotenuse}\)

Cosine: \(cos\theta = \frac{adjacent}{hypotenuse}\)

Tangent: \(tan\theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}\)

Cotangent: \(cot\theta = \frac{adjacent}{opposite}\)

Secant: \(sec\theta = \frac{hypotenuse}{adjacent}\)

Cosecant: \(csc\theta = \frac{hypotenuse}{opposite}\)

Example Problems

Problem 1: Find the sine of an angle in a right triangle with an opposite side of 6 and a hypotenuse of 10.

Solution: Using the sine formula:

\(sin\theta = \frac{opposite}{hypotenuse} = \frac{6}{10} = 0.6\)

Problem 2: Determine the cotangent of an angle in a right triangle with an adjacent side of 8 and an opposite side of 6.

Solution: Using the cotangent formula:

\(cot\theta = \frac{adjacent}{opposite} = \frac{8}{6} = 1.33\)
